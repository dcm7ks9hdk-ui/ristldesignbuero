Profile

I am Pascal Ristl, a trained cabinetmaker. My apprenticeship taught me to work methodically from the first measurement to the finished piece: understanding material, constructing joints, thinking through each step in the right order. That closeness to material and making still shapes how I approach every design task.

Over time it became clear that I did not just want to build — I wanted to design. Since the winter semester of 2025 I have been studying interior architecture at TH Rosenheim. The programme widens my perspective beyond wood: other materials, the fundamentals of building construction, and the tools of design — CAD and modelling software, colour and typography, composition, representation and presentation.

The two perspectives complement each other. Knowing how something is built changes how you design it: more precisely, truer to the material, closer to execution. In turn, my studies give me the tools to develop, test and communicate ideas before the first board is cut. This portfolio documents that path — from detail to space.
